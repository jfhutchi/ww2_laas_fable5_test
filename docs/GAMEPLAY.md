# Gameplay Guide

## Objective chain

1. **Advance into the village** up the southern approach road (artillery-cratered, covered by a PaK 40 sited on the road shoulder).
2. **Neutralize the defenders** — grenadier squads at building corners, two MG-42 teams with prepared firing lanes, the PaK, and a StuG III lurking north-east.
3. **Enter the village center** and stand inside the flagged capture ring at the crossroads.
4. **Capture** — the bar fills while your units hold the ring uncontested. Combined arms (tanks + infantry together) capture ~35% faster; scouts alone capture slowly.
5. **Survive the counterattack** — at 50% progress, two Panzer IVs and two grenadier squads push down the north road.
6. **Hold** until *Securing* completes → **Mission accomplished**.

Capture states: `Neutral → Capturing → (Contested) → Securing → Secured`, with `Enemy Recapturing` when the zone is lost with progress on the bar. Contested freezes progress; leaving the zone lets it decay slowly.

## Combat model

- **Armor facing**: front/side/rear armor classes; penetration falls off with range; grazing frontal hits can ricochet. Penetrations roll crits: mobility damage, turret jam, fires (burning does damage over time).
- **Cover**: walls, hedgerows, buildings, rubble, wrecks and craters reduce hit chance, blast damage and suppression. Infantry automatically scramble to nearby cover when suppressed in the open.
- **Suppression**: MG bursts and HE pin infantry — pinned troops crawl and return only sporadic fire. Cover speeds recovery.
- **Line of sight**: buildings and bocage block sight; smoke (fires, shell impacts) degrades it; ridges matter. Enemies render only while genuinely spotted — or briefly when their muzzle flash betrays them.
- **Wrecks** persist: they block vehicles, give infantry cover, and burn.

## Tactics that work

- Lead with scouts on the flanks; keep tanks hull-toward the PaK's lane.
- Shell the MG buildings with attack-ground (G) before walking rifles in.
- The bocage is vehicle-proof: hedgerow gates and the roads are your armor axes — infantry can push through gaps.
- When the counterattack triggers, get your Shermans hull-down behind wrecks/walls at the north exits and let the Panzers come to you.
- Direct control (Tab) makes a Sherman noticeably deadlier at long range — the AI won't lead its shots down a road the way you can.
